Module 1 · 问题与动机

Carbon-dot-modified hydroxylated BN is embedded into a silk hydrogel patch to balance thermal conduction, UV handling, biodegradability and skin-contact cooling.

Why it matters

Wearable cooling needs a matrix that is biocompatible, degradable and thermally useful.

Main bottleneck

Many conductive fillers are good conductors but poor skin-contact materials without surface engineering.

Core idea

Hydroxylated BN is further modified by carbon quantum dots to improve phonon transport and UV response.

Module 4 · 本文方法详解

Method flow

  1. 1. Prepare CDs Make carbon quantum dots from urea/citrate precursors.
  2. 2. Build CDs@BNOH Functionalize hydroxylated BN with CDs to tune the interface.
  3. 3. Make SilMA Methacrylate-modify silk fibroin for UV-curable gelation.
  4. 4. Infuse patch Load the filler into cotton-supported gel and cure into a wearable patch.

Key formula

Proper CDs modification improves phonon transport; too much coating disrupts the BN network.
  • fwhm · Raman linewidth used as a phonon-lifetime indicator
  • D% · degradation fraction of the hydrogel
  • ΔT · surface cooling effect relative to blank
  • λ · patch thermal conductivity

Module 6 · 局限与展望

Current limitations

  • Conductivity is still below the best film-type BNNS systems.
  • Outdoor performance depends on contact and ambient conditions.
  • Long-term wear tests are still limited.

Future work

  • Quantify comfort under sweat, motion and repeated bending.
  • Optimize CDs loading to keep the BN phonon network intact.
  • Explore device integration for wearable electronics.
  • Test other biodegradable matrices and patch geometries.
