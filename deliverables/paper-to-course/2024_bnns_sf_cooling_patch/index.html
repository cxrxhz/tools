Module 1 · 问题与动机

BNNS and silk fibroin are combined into an aligned, reusable cooling film that balances heat spreading, radiative cooling and skin compatibility.

Why it matters

Cooling is energy intensive, so passive skin-contact cooling is a practical target.

Main bottleneck

Pure silk is biocompatible but not conductive enough for rapid heat dissipation.

Core idea

Use BNNS as a directional heat spreader and silk fibroin as a durable matrix.

Module 4 · 本文方法详解

Method flow

  1. 1. Prepare BNNS Exfoliate h-BN into nanosheets without losing the platelet structure.
  2. 2. Mix with SF Combine with silk fibroin before vacuum filtration.
  3. 3. Build alignment Layer-by-layer stacking forms a directional thermal path.
  4. 4. Validate Measure thermal conductivity, reflectance, emissivity and biosafety.

Key formula

λ = α × ρ × Cp; TCE = (λcomposite - λSF) / λSF × 100%
  • λ · effective thermal conductivity
  • α · thermal diffusivity
  • ρ · density
  • Cp · specific heat capacity

Module 6 · 局限与展望

Current limitations

  • Very high filler loading may reduce softness.
  • Large-area alignment is harder to scale.
  • Wear, sweat and washing need broader field testing.

Future work

  • Optimize interfacial chemistry to reduce phonon scattering.
  • Develop roll-to-roll or other scalable alignment routes.
  • Test laundering and fatigue under realistic clothing conditions.
  • Co-design with textile architectures for all-day comfort.
